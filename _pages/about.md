I'm a Ph.D. candidate in the deparment of Mechanical Engineering at Stanford. My research interests are in wall-modeled large-eddy simulations (WMLES), turbulence, numerical methods development, and hypersonics. My work is done in Professor Parviz Moin's group in the Center for Turbulence Research.

As a Hispanic in a PhD program, I believe it is important that I do my part and give back to the community to help put other Hispanics in a position to succeed. I am a member of Stanford's El Centro Chicano y Latino where I participate in an undergrad mentorship program. I'm also the financial officer for seeME, an outreach club in the department of Mechanical Engineering whose aim is to introduce local underrepresented middle school and high school students to STEM and engineering.

When I'm not working on my research, I have three main hobbies. I'm a huge basketball fan, both watching the NBA and playing myself. I also love playing video games, both immersive single-player games as well as competitive FPS games. Most recently, I've been working on becoming a better cook.
